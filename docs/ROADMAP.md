# Sustainable Catalyst Decision Studio Roadmap

## Current release

### v2.0.0 — Connected Decision Intelligence Platform

- Connect the full decision lifecycle from framing through reassessment.
- Assess twelve lifecycle stages and expose missing records, blockers, and responsible products.
- Route research, evidence, modeling, publication, implementation, monitoring, and registry work across the Sustainable Catalyst platform.
- Build a Decision Intelligence Graph from packet evidence, models, alternatives, risks, publications, entities, and reassessments.

## Post-v2 priorities

Future releases should concentrate on production findings rather than precommitting to feature accumulation:

- Live integration validation across Platform Core, Knowledge Library, Research Librarian, Site Intelligence, Workbench, Research Lab, Contact and Engagement, Publications, and Channel
- Performance and accessibility findings from real institutional packets
- Governance and publication workflow refinements based on human review
- Migration, backup, restore, and disaster-recovery exercises
- Security, privacy, observability, and API lifecycle maintenance
- Carefully scoped domain packs and exchange adapters where institutional demand is demonstrated

## Architectural boundaries

- Knowledge Library supplies durable sources and citations and receives reviewed publication handoffs.
- Research Librarian routes research and evidence gaps.
- Site Intelligence supplies live, comparative, and monitoring evidence with methodology and freshness context.
- Workbench performs calculations, simulations, optimization, and technical analysis.
- Research Lab supplies experimental and scientific artifacts.
- Platform Core supplies shared identity, provenance, Evidence Ledger, Decision Registry, events, and exchange contracts.
- Decision Studio orchestrates alternatives, governance, approval, publication, implementation, monitoring, reassessment, and accountability.
- Connected routes are structured handoffs and do not claim external acceptance or execution.
- Automated assessment never replaces human approval, professional judgment, required assurance, security review, or accessibility testing.
